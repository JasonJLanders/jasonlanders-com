About Jason

I'm the Director of Named Account Sales Engineering at Datadog, where I lead the SE organization covering the company's largest enterprise accounts across North America.

I've spent 20+ years at the intersection of engineering and enterprise sales — starting as a Software Engineer at Microsoft and working my way through product management, individual SE work, and team leadership at companies including Mandiant, Rapid7, Malwarebytes, Splunk, and Datadog.

Right now, the work I find most interesting is figuring out how AI changes the SE function in practice — building new workflows with my team, rethinking what we hire for, and trying to separate what actually matters from the noise. I write about what I learn, including the things that don't go as planned.

In 2016, I founded Landrian, a security startup that debuted at RSA Conference and was later covered by Forrester in their "2017 Top Technology Trends to Watch" report. The technology was ultimately acquired and integrated into a product deployed by the Department of Defense.
